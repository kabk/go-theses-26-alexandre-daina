This project explores the relationship between Hyper-Awareness, Isolation, and Perceptual Distortion through a narrative essay structured in three movements. It follows Tristan, initially accompanied by a psychologist, to examine how excessive self-perception, first experienced as control and precision, gradually destabilises his ability to remain oriented within reality.

In Paris, Hyper-Awareness is constructed as a heightened sensitivity to social space. Tristan constantly perceives himself within situations, anticipating gestures, correcting speech, and adjusting his presence. This produces a doubling, where a “Second Tristan” observes and evaluates the first. What appears as precision progressively turns inward, becoming continuous self-surveillance.

The transition to Isolation unfolds through the journey to the castle. As external interactions diminish, the friction that previously grounded perception disappears. Tristan’s awareness no longer adjusts to others, but expands without resistance.

In the castle, this process intensifies. The space remains stable, yet small discrepancies accumulate: echoes extend, reflections lag, and repeated actions become absorbing. Observation begins to precede experience, as notes describe Tristan before he acts. The distinction between observing and being observed collapses.
This culminates in the emergence of a presence following him, initially ambiguous, then revealed as his own image, misaligned and delayed. What appears as an external threat is inseparable from his own perception.

The project constructs a perceptual trajectory through the essay, later translated into film. It suggests that the risk lies in excess, when Perception folds inward and can no longer distinguish between what is produced and what is perceived.
